Essay Topics

  • How does Tom's character evolve over the novel?
  • At the beginning of the novel Tom is a very mischievous young boy who is focused on one thing, amusing himself and having fun.
  • Tom proves to be a very intuitive kid and very understanding of human nature, however he uses his intellect to focus on playing tricks on people and other games.
  • He leads a carefree childhood, however one day that all changes.
  • Tom and Huck witness Injun Joe murder a man, and from then on Tom's character is in for a dramatic shift.
  • He becomes a much more serious person focusing on more important things such as personal safety, as well as the needs of others such as Muff Potter, who Tom puts his life on the line for by defending his name.
  • Later on while Tom and Becky are lost in the cave Tom once again risks his life in order to save Becky.
  • Tom becomes much more mature throughout the novel, as well as begins to realize his priorities.
  • Tom goes from being a child to becoming a teenager on his way towards adulthood.
  • However one thing that does not change is Tom's upbeat and charismatic personality, as despite his maturation he does not lose his playfulness and humor.
  • Describe Aunt Polly and Tom's relationship.
  • Throughout the novel Tom and Aunt Polly go through a real effort to show the reader that they are foes.
  • As Tom seems to always be up to some sort of trouble, meanwhile Polly is always waiting to punish him for his missteps.
  • Despite their constant efforts to derail each others way of life, they seem to show they are fond of one another though neither are willing to admit it.
  • Aunt Polly struggles to truly put forth any harsh punishment for Tom's actions, the worst being when she is tricked into believing Tom has died.
  • Meanwhile Tom shows his affection in a letter he writes to Aunt Polly to comfort her after she believes he is dead.
  • In the end it is clear that despite their hard stance against the other, it is more out of love than anything else.
