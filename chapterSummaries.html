Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • The novel begins and Aunt Polly is searching through her house for Tom Sawyer, her nephew
  • Tom is hiding in the closet, hands covered in jam, leaving Aunt Polly upset and preparing for a beating
  • Before the beating occurs Tom shouts "look behind you!" causing Aunt Polly to turn, and Tom to have time to escape outside and over the fence
  • Aunt Polly is fuming and upset about how she has allowed him to get away with far too much
  • Tom eventually returns home that night around dinner time to help Jim, Aunt Polly's slave chop wood, he tells Jim about his adventures of the day
  • At dinner Aunt Polly questions Tom, she believes he skipped school to go swimming and wants to get to the bottom of it
  • Tom denies it saying that his hair is wet because he poured water on it, he also says that there is no way he went swimming as his collar is sewn down and there's no way he could have taken it off to swim
  • His half brother Sid points out that the thread is now black, not white like the morning, so he must have gone swimming
  • Tom leaves furiously but quickly puts the incident behind him and begins to roam the streets of St. Petersburg practicing a new type of whistling
  • Tom comes across a young boy about his age who appears arrogant to Tom, they exchange insults and then wrestling
  • Tom wins the exchange and then chases him all the way home
  • Tom eventually returns home and is confronted by Aunt Polly, she sees his dirty clothes and is upset, she tells him he will be working the next day, which is a Saturday
  • Chapter 2
  • The next morning Tom is ordered to white wash the fence, and while white washing the fence Jim walks by and Tom makes a proposal, Jim will help white wash in exchange for a marble
  • Aunt Polly sees this exchange and chases Jim off
  • Later a young boy passes by and Tom convinces him that white washing is fun and that he should trade an apple in order to have the pleasure of white washing
  • By the end of the day the white washing is done and Tom has convinced many boys to do the work while they give Tom trinkets as well, Tom ends the day very pleased with what he has done
  • Chapter 3
  • Aunt Polly is thrilled with Tom's work and lets him go out that afternoon
  • Tom makes sure to pelt clods of dirt at Sid when he sees him, for his betrayal at dinner, on his way downtown
  • When he gets down town he and his friend Joe Harper help some other boys stage a mock battle while Tom and Joe act as generals, Tom wins the battle
  • Tom returns home and on his way he passes by the Thatcher house where he sees a beautiful girl and falls in love immediately, forgetting his last crush
  • Tom begins to show off for her and eventually she tosses him a flower before he returns home
  • At dinner that night Sid breaks a sugar bowl and blames Tom
  • Tom is upset and leaves again, he is very melodramatic and thinks no one would care if he died
  • He returns to the beautiful girls house where plans to die pitifully under her window
  • However a maid opens it and dumps a bucket of water on his head
  • He sadly returns home and goes to sleep
  • Chapter 4
  • It is Sunday and Tom is getting ready for Sunday school, yet he is not prepared for the bible verses he was supposed to memorize
  • His cousin Mary absolves to help him encouraging him with a bribe if he memorizes them, when he does she gives him a "barlow" knife, and he, Mary, and Sid go to Sunday school
  • During Sunday school Tom trades the trinkets he received with white washing for tickets, the tickets are earned by memorizing verses and if 2000 are collected by someone they receive a Bible awarded to them in front of the class
  • During class Judge Thatcher, a judge and Tom's friend Jeff's uncle, visits
  • The Judge's daughter Becky is with him, she turns out to be the girl Tom saw the day before
  • Tom tries to show off again by presenting all the collected tickets for the bible, his teacher is aware that there is no way he memorized all those verses but decides he must go through with the bible ceremony because he wants to impress the Judge
  • The Judge is amazed by Tom's accomplishment and asks him to recite the 12 disciples to the class, however Tom is unable to and replies with David and Goliath, obviously he is wrong
  • Chapter 5
  • Following Sunday school Tom attends church service, which is a long and tedious sermon speaking of how Christ will reign again over earth and the lion and lamb will lie down together and a child should lead them
  • Tom wishes that he could be the child to do that if the lion were tame
  • Tom is bored during the service and decides to open a box he has with him and inside there is a pinch bug
  • The bug escapes and bites a stray dog that wandered into church causing the dog to yelp and cause a ruckus
  • The service is ended shortly after that and Tom returns home happily
  • Chapter 6
  • That Monday Tom desperately tries to fake sick to skip school yet this does not happen
  • He is forced to go and on his way he comes across Huckleberry Finn, the son of the town drunk
  • All the mothers of the town want their children to avoid Huck believing he is a bad influence, however all the boys envy him because he can skip school and not recieve any punishment
  • The boys discuss ways to remove warts and Huck explains (he is carrying a dead cat) that taking a dead cat to the cemetery at night will allow the devil to come and take the dead cat away with a dead persons soul, and the dead cat will take the warts away with it
  • Tom wants to witness this so he agrees to go with Huck that night to the cemetery
  • Tom is late to the school and demands an explanation
  • Tom, knowing he will be punished and forced to sit with the girls, Becky Thatcher in particular, tells the teacher he was speaking to Huck
  • The teacher sure enough whips him and makes him sit by Becky
  • Tom takes advantage of this time to flirt with Becky and the two get to know each other
  • He eventually writes 'I love you' on his slate to her, the teacher notices and makes him go sit with the boys again
  • Chapter 7
  • Tom is now sitting by Joe Harper and the boys begin to play with a tick, eventually getting in trouble and receiving a whack on the head from the teacher
  • At lunch Tom stays inside with Becky, and the two continue to flirt
  • Tom convinces her to "get engaged" with him and they both say they love the other, even exchanging a kiss
  • Tom is so excited he accidentally mentions he was previously engaged to Amy Lawrence
  • This upsets Becky and she begins to cry, Tom tries to deny it and say he only loves Becky, but this causes even more problems
  • Eventually Tom leaves and Becky is hysterical
  • Chapter 8
  • Tom goes to the forest where he wanders about for the afternoon
  • He decides he will become a pirate, and while imaging this Joe Harper encounters him, and the two boys play Robin Hood
  • After playing the both agree that being an outlaw for a year would be so much more fulfilling then being the president for a lifetime
  • Chapter 9
  • That night Tom sneaks out to meet Huck in the graveyard, while there they witness three adults in the graveyard as well
  • Dr. Robinson, Muff Potter, and Injun Joe are the three men
  • The Dr. orders the men to dig up Hoss Williams' grave, and the men then begin to quarell as Potter wants more money, and Joe has a grudge against Dr. Robinson
  • A fight ensues and Dr. Robinson knocks out Potter shortly before being stabbed by Injun Joe
  • The boys flee the graveyard. Following the fight Potter awakens to see the crime scene, Injun Joe tells Potter, who is considerably drunk, that Potter stabbed Joe, and that he would not tell anyone
  • As they both leave it is noted that Potter's knife is stuck inside the Dr.
  • Chapter 10
  • The boys escape to a tannery where they hide out, they are afraid of the consequences of telling on Injun Joe and decide to swear to each other that they will never tell anyone
  • The boys then hear dog's howling and think it is towards them and that perhaps that means they will die
  • However when they go outside they see the dogs are barking at Potter
  • Tom sneaks back home and is noticed by Sid who tells Aunt Polly the next day
  • She is very upset with Tom and when Tom leaves for school he is very distraught, and once at school a trinket he tried to give Becky has been returned to his desk leaving him even more upset
  • Chapter 11
  • The next day Dr. Robinson's body is found and many townspeople begin talking
  • Injun Joe goes on to tell the sheriff that it was Muff Potter who killed the Dr. and once Potter's knife is found he is taken to jail
  • Tom is very conflicted and is afraid to tell the truth
  • He does however sneak out to Potter's cell and gives him small gifts often
  • Chapter 12
  • Becky Thatcher becomes sick and starts missing school causing Tom to fall into a deep depression
  • Aunt Polly finally begins to worry about Tom and after trying to treat his "illness" she decides to give him a terrible tasting serum to make him feel better
  • This doesn't work and Tom hates the taste so he feeds it to the cat causing the cat to act crazy
  • Aunt Polly realizes that if it seemed to be cruelty to the cat it probably was to Tom as well and feeling bad she sends him back to school without punishing him
  • Becky returns to school as well but completely ignores Tom
  • Chapter 13
  • Tom decides he wants to become a pirate so he goes and convinces Joe Harper to join him who obliges willingly because his mother is upset at him
  • The boys also bring Huck along and they decide to go to Jackson's Island, a small, forested isle a few miles down the St. Petersburg river
  • The boys raft down the river and arrive at the isle in the middle of the night, they proceed to build a fire and cook stolen bacon
  • However they quickly feel bad about stealing and in turn vow to never steal again as pirates
  • Chapter 14
  • The boys wake up to see that their raft is missing, they are relieved because they are free from land entirely and they then decide to go fishing and have a wonderful catch
  • However the first afternoon they become homesick
  • While sitting on the shore they see boats on the river, probably searching for them and thinking they have drowned
  • The boys are actually excited about the thought of that as they feel like heroes
  • That night the boys discuss leaving but Tom shoots down the idea
  • However Tom does decide to go back to the mainland in the middle of the night and see what people's reactions are to them missing
  • He leaves a note for Joe and then crosses the river
  • Chapter 15
  • Tom sneaks back into his house and hides under a bed listening to Aunt Polly, Sid, Mary, and Mrs. Harper
  • The group talks about missing the boys and wishing they were perhaps kinder to them
  • Tom also hears them discuss the search crew finding there raft and assuming they drowned
  • Tom thinks about leaving a letter for his aunt but decides against it, he then returns to the island where the boys cook breakfast and Tom tells what he heard
  • Chapter 16
  • The boys enjoy meals of turtle eggs, go swimming, wrestle, and play circus on the beach, but eventually again succumb to homesickness
  • Joe and Huck decide to return home but Tom convinces them to stay with a secret plan
  • Huck teaches Tom and Joe how to smoke pipes, and the boys are at first thrilled but both become sick and run into the forest and vomit and sleep
  • Later that day Huck plans to prepare for them to smoke again but both decline on account of food sickness
  • That night a huge storm hits the island destroying there camp and tent, they manage to rebuild a fire and some of their camp somewhat rejuvenating there spirits
  • The boys then play Indians and Tom and Joe decide to smoke again because you can not be an Indian without a peace pipe
  • Chapter 17
  • Back in town everyone is in morning, including Becky Thatcher who feels as if she should have treated Tom better
  • That Sunday the community gathers for the funeral and the minister gives a wonderful sermon and everyone is left wondering how they overlooked the goodness in the two boys, Joe and Tom
  • The entire church is moved to tears, and at that point all three boys enter shocking everyone
  • Tom and Joe are received warmly and embraced by there families but Huck is left alone which saddens Tom
  • The congregation is grateful and sings "Old Hundred"
  • Chapter 18
  • The day after the funeral Aunt Polly is a little perturbed with Tom as he had let her think he was dead, however he says he couldn't let her know he was still alive because it would ruin the adventure
  • He also mentions that he had visions of everyone back in town, and tells of when he snuck in the house and listened to everyone talking however he says that was a vision and does not mention he was there, this does however leave Sid suspicious
  • Tom returns to school a hero and he begins to ignore Becky and renews his relationship with Amy Lawrence
  • Becky is upset so she makes sure to invite the entire class to a picnic except Tom and Amy
  • Tom and Amy are not bothered by this and Becky becomes very jealous
  • Becky begins talking to another boy, Alfred Temple, who Tom fought at the beginning of the novel
  • But she soon drops him as she misses Tom and Alfred takes revenge on Tom by pouring ink on Tom's spelling book, getting Tom in trouble
  • Becky knew about what Alfred does but decides not to warn Tom and ultimately decides she will hate Tom forever
  • Chapter 19
  • Aunt Polly learns that Tom's vision was a lie and scolds the boy because she is upset that he didn't let them know he was alive when he snuck back in
  • Tom tells her that he planned to leave a note but he did not want to ruin the surprise, and that the note is in his jacket pocket
  • Aunt Polly searches his pockets and finds it, so moved by the note she resolves that she could forgive all of his sins
  • Chapter 20
  • Tom returns to school planning to reconcile with Becky but she does not want any of it
  • Later that day Becky sneaks into the teachers desk and finds an anatomy book that he hides because he is studying to be a doctor in his spare time
  • Tom walks in on Becky looking at it and she accidentally rips a page, Becky is very upset as she knows she will receive a whipping for it
  • When the teacher sees the rip he searches the class for the perpetrator, but when he gets to Becky, Tom takes the blame, and again he is a hero in her eyes
  • Chapter 21
  • The school year is nearing an end and Mr. Dobbins has become much more frequent with his abuse of the children
  • The examinations approach and the children must present speeches and poems, and partake in geography and spelling quizes, Tom is assigned to remember and recite "Give me Liberty or Give me Death" yet is overcome with stage fright
  • There then is a series of female students who recite hilariously awful poems
  • The children prepared a blind-folded cat, tied to a string, and hung from the rafters to provide amusement
  • As the teacher is drawing on the black board the cat is lowered down and claws at the air and ultimately clawing of Mr. Dobbins wig revealing his bald head which had been painted gold by a student while Mr. Dobbins had been sleeping off a drunk spell
  • Chapter 22
  • During summer Tom decides to join the Cadets of Temperance, his reasoning being that they have cool uniforms
  • Tom must give up smoking, chewing, and cursing however to be a part of the Cadets, which is very difficult to him
  • Tom plans to quit but decides to wait until after Judge Frazier dies so that he might get to wear a red sash at the funeral
  • The judge makes a recovery so Tom quits, but the day after quiting, the Judge suddenly dies in the night
  • Becky has gone on vacation and Tom is left very bored, the murder of Dr. Robinson still weighs greatly on his mind
  • Tom succumbs to the measles and is in bed, after recovering he realizes that everyone in town has become very religious and left there sinful ways
  • A thunderstorm occurs one night, and Tom assumes it is punishment for his sins
  • He has a relapse of measles and spends another 3 weeks in bed, when he returns to normal he realizes everyone has relapsed there Christian ways and returned to their sinful ways
  • Chapter 23
  • It becomes time for Muff Potter's trial and the boys are very conflicted as to what they should do
  • The trial begins not boding well for Muff but Tom is suprisingly called to the stand
  • He recounts what he witnessed and Injun Joe, who was attending the trial, escapes from the court room out a window
  • Chapter 24
  • Tom is considered a hero, and enjoys how he is treated
  • However he can not get over the fear that Injun Joe will come kill him
  • Joe has vanished and no one can track him down
  • Chapter 25
  • Tom and Huck decide one day that they want to search for treasure, so they go looking for dead trees where they believe someone would hide treasure
  • The boys discuss what they would do with the treasure, and Huck states he would buy sweets and soda, while Tom likes the idea of getting married with his treasure, Huck thinks Tom's idea is ridiculous
  • The boys did and dig but find nothing which frustrates them, they realize that perhaps they need to wait to midnight and dig again where the shadow of the tree limb falls
  • The boys return at night but again have no luck, frustrated, they decide to go to the haunted house, which is an abandon building nearby
  • Chapter 26
  • So on the following morning they plan to finally go to the haunted house, but after realizing it is a Friday, the unluckiest day of the week, the boys decide to wait till Saturday, and instead play Robin Hood
  • On Saturday the boys explore the haunted house finally
  • While upstairs in the house, the boys witness two men walk through the front door, one man is a deaf and mute Spaniard who had been hanging around town
  • However the mute Spaniard speaks, now that he is alone, and the boys realize it is Injun Joe
  • The boys are scared to death, and wait quietly, hoping the men don't notice them
  • The men talk of criminal activities, and then fall asleep, however the boys are still to afraid to leave
  • The next morning the men plan to hide $600 that they had stolen by burying it in the house, however they notice some tools, the boys tools, and they realize that someone must be in the house
  • Injun Joe decides to head upstairs and search and see if anybody is there, the stairs collapse under Joe's weight, and the two men decide to leave and bury the treasure elsewhere
  • The boys finally leave as well, upset that they left there tools and had no opportunity to steal the treasure
  • The boys still plan to follow Joe in hopes to get the treasure, they are however worried that Joe will still seek revenge on Tom
  • Chapter 27
  • Tom awakes the next day thinking that perhaps all that what happened might have been a dream, but after being told by Huck it was not, the boys decide to search for the treasure
  • The boys are searching for a place that they heard the men the night before described as "Number Two"
  • The boys decipher that it must mean that it is room number two in a tavern in town
  • After finding the room they believe to be the correct one, they begin trying to open it while keeping an eye out for Injun Joe
  • Chapter 28
  • While in the tavern and keeping watch on the room Tom notices Injun Joe inside, passed out and drunk on whiskey
  • The two boys run away afraid for their lives, they do come up with a plan though that one will watch the room for Joe to leave and then Tom will sneak in and steal the treasure
  • Chapter 29
  • Becky finally returns home and the following day will be her picnic
  • The picnic will be at a famous cavern down river, and Becky has been told by her mother to spend the night at a friends house near the river
  • Tom convinces her to come spend the night with him at Widow Douglas' house
  • The time comes to take the ferry down river for the picnic, and Tom puts thoughts of Injun Joe beside to spend time with becky
  • The children arrive at the cavern where they play, eat lunch, and explore a cave, all before happily heading home
  • Huck is waiting by for the ferry when he sees two men pass by carrying a box, he realizes it to be Injun Joe and his friend
  • He follows them to the Widow's house, where Joe plans to exact revenge on her because her husband had once had Joe horsewhipped
  • Huck realizes what is going to happen so he rushes to the house of a Welshman and tells the family what's about to happen, the father and his sons rush to the Widow's house and saves her life
  • Chapter 30
  • The next day Huck returns to the Welshman's house where he learns that Injun Joe was not captured, but instead chased away, so Huck explains that the "Spaniard" was really Injun Joe
  • Huck also learns that the box the men were carrying was only burglary tools, meaning the money was still waiting to be found
  • The Welshman does not tell everyone who the boy was that saved the widow, as he plans to wait, that morning at Church Mrs. Thatcher realizes Becky is missing and Aunt Polly realizes Tom is missing
  • They determine they must still be at the cave
  • The townspeople search for Becky and Tom but turn up nothing other then a carving of there names in the cave
  • Back in town it is discovered that the tavern where Joe had been staying serves liquor, which it was not allowed to, and the tavern is shut down
  • Huck learns of this and assumes the treasure is gone
  • Tom and Becky are still missing and have been for a few days
  • Chapter 31
  • The story goes back to Tom and Becky, and we learn that they left the other children in the caves and went to explore, however they were attacked by bats and got lost
  • When the search crew came they did not hear the children call out and were still lost
  • Tom tries to explore and find a way out of the cave while Becky rests
  • While searching for a way out he sees a lit candle in the distance and realizes it is Injun Joe
  • Tom is afraid and runs away, he decides not to tell Becky and instead search for a different passage
  • Chapter 32
  • Tom and Becky finally escape the cave late in the middle of a Tuesday night
  • Tom was searching and saw a speck of light, after walking towards it he was able to escape out of a small hole with Becky
  • The two find a house 5 miles downstream from the cave
  • Becky and Tom are bedridden for many days after they escape the cave
  • When Tom is finally healthy enough he goes to see Huck and learns about Injun Joe and his attempt to kill the widow
  • Joe's accomplice apparently died when he drowned in the river
  • Two weeks after the cave incident Tom talks to Judge Thatcher and learns the cave has been shut off so no one will ever get lost again
  • He is horrified and tells the Judge that Joe is still down there
  • Chapter 33
  • A search party returns to the cave where they find Injun Joe dead, he had starved to death after eating a few bats
  • A funeral is held for him and following Tom and Huck decide to go search for the treasure again because they believe it to be in the cave
  • Once in the cave the boys search and eventually come across a cross etched into the wall where they figure the treasure to be
  • The boys dig under some rocks and eventually find some guns, moccasins, a belt, and the treasure
  • The boys leave the guns and keep the treasure and while on there way rafting back to St. Petersburg they come across the Welshman who invites them to a party at the widow's house
  • Chapter 34
  • The party is for the Welshman and his sons for saving the widow's life
  • Every important person from the village has arrived for the party and Huck feels out of place and wants to leave
  • The Welshman plans to announce to the party that it was in fact Huck who is responsible for saving the widow
  • Sid has apparently ruined the surprise and Tom becomes infuriated and chases him down
  • At dinner it is announced that Huck will be taken care of and given a home
  • Tom announces that Huck doesn't need that because he is rich, he then proceeds to run outside and grab the treasure
  • The treasure turns out to be over 12 thousand dollars
  • Chapter 35
  • The money is invested and both boys are set up with a very nice allowance of a dollar a day, the same salary as the minister
  • Becky's father the Judge, decides Tom should become a lawyer after attending the National Military Academy
  • While Huck hates the idea of being civilized, wearing clean clothes, and sleeping in sheets
  • Huck eventually runs away, and Tom is the only person who can find him, as he is hidden in a slaughter house because he hates his new life
  • Tom convinces Huck to return home because Tom can't have a gang member in his gang of robbers that is not respectable
  • Huck returns home on the condition that he will be a part of Tom's gang
